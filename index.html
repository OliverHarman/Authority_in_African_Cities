Authority in African Cities

RAI(Metro) Explorer · 7 countries · 1977–2025

African cities are growing rapidly, yet systematic data on metropolitan authority remain absent from comparative datasets. This explorer presents the first cross-country, time-varying measure of metropolitan authority in Africa using the Regional Authority Index (RAI) framework. Analysing constitutional and legislative texts from 1990 to 2025 across seven countries—Ghana, Kenya, Malawi, Rwanda, Sierra Leone, Tanzania, and Uganda—it measures both self-rule (local authority to act) and shared-rule (local voice in national decisions) across multiple institutional dimensions. Select a country or measure to explore how formal metropolitan authority has evolved.
